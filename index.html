Aryaman Gupta

Hi, I’m Aryaman, a Ph.D. candidate at Stanford University, advised by Prof. Somil Bansal. I earned my B.Tech. in Electronics Engineering from IIT (BHU) Varanasi.

My research focuses on uncovering failure patterns in robotic systems to enable a continuous failure-guided improvement cycle. I aim to: (a) bake safety mechanisms into their design, and (b) ensure reliable operation at runtime through lightweight guardrails.

Get an overview of my research, grab my resume, or find my work on Google Scholar. Feel free to reach out — I’m always up for discussions on robotics, AI, and tech in general.

  3. ASOC
    Deep Reinforcement Learning for Sim-to-Real Policy Transfer of VTOL-UAVs Offshore Docking Operations
    Ali Mohamed Ali, Aryaman Gupta, and Hashim A. Hashim
    Applied Soft Computing, 2024
    Abs DOI Bib HTML Code

    This paper proposes a novel Reinforcement Learning (RL) approach for sim-to-real policy transfer of Vertical Take-Off and Landing Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (VTOL-UAV). The proposed approach is designed for VTOL-UAV landing on offshore docking stations in maritime operations. The landing procedure is decomposed into an approach phase, handled by a model-based control scheme, and a landing phase, where DRL agents are trained offline to learn the optimal docking policy. The JONSWAP spectrum model generates a wave model for each episode to enhance policy generalization for sim2real transfer. Numerical experiments show that the PPO agent learns efficient policies to land in uncertain environments, enhancing the likelihood of successful sim-to-real transfer.
