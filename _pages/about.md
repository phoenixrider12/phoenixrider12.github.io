Hi, I'm Aryaman! I am a PhD student at [Stanford University](https://www.stanford.edu/) in the Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics, where I feel grateful to be advised by [Prof. Somil Bansal](https://smlbansal.github.io/) in the [SIA Lab](https://smlbansal.github.io/sia-lab/index.html). Prior to Stanford, I spent four wonderful years at IIT (BHU) Varanasi earning my B.Tech. in Electronics Engineering.

My research focuses on learning from deployment-time data to uncover failure patterns in robotic systems for continuous improvement. I aim to: (a) bake safety mechanisms into their design, and (b) ensure reliable and efficient operation at runtime through lightweight guardrails.

You can read a short overview of my research [here](/assets/pdf/Aryaman_Research_Overview.pdf), grab my [resume](/assets/pdf/Resume.pdf), or find my work on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=AWhTyqwAAAAJ&hl=en). Feel free to reach out — I'm always happy to chat about robotics, safety, and learning-based control.
